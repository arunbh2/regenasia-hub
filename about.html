About RegenAsia Hub

Connecting knowledge, communities, and resources for climate-resilient food systems

Our Mission

To transform agriculture across South and Southeast Asia by connecting practitioners, experts, communities, and funders to build climate-resilient food systems through regenerative practices.

The agriculture and food systems across South and South-East Asia—particularly in Indonesia, Vietnam, the Philippines, Thailand, Bangladesh, India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka—are increasingly vulnerable to the accelerating impacts of climate change. Farmers and local communities regularly face unprecedented challenges such as unpredictable rainfall patterns, prolonged droughts, severe flooding, loss of biodiversity, soil degradation, and reduced productivity.

The RegenAsia Hub serves as a comprehensive platform that bridges the significant knowledge gap and fragmentation in current regenerative agriculture initiatives and climate resilience efforts across the region. We address the challenges faced by local organizations, communities, and agricultural practitioners who struggle with limited access to practical knowledge, effective tools, funding opportunities, and collaborative networks needed to scale regenerative practices.

Our platform brings together diverse stakeholders, including local communities, NGOs, private sector partners, funding organizations, research institutions, and independent professionals, fostering a vibrant community of practice focused on regenerative agriculture and climate resilience.

Our Geographic Focus

RegenAsia Hub concentrates on eight countries in South and Southeast Asia that are particularly vulnerable to climate change impacts on agriculture:

Bangladesh

Coastal and delta nation facing severe flood risks, sea level rise, cyclones, and salinization of agricultural lands.

India

Home to a vast agricultural landscape facing challenges of erratic monsoons, soil degradation, and water scarcity.

Nepal

Mountain farming systems vulnerable to changing rainfall patterns, glacial melt, and extreme weather events.

Sri Lanka

Island nation confronting intensifying droughts, floods, and risks to traditional agricultural systems.

Indonesia

Archipelago facing sea level rise, changing rainfall patterns, and pressures on agricultural land.

Vietnam

Coastal and delta farming areas vulnerable to sea intrusion, typhoons, and seasonal flooding.

Philippines

Nation regularly affected by typhoons and extreme weather damaging crops and agricultural infrastructure.

Thailand

Regional agricultural producer experiencing droughts, floods, and changes in traditional growing patterns.

Governance & Advisory

RegenAsia Hub is guided by a diverse Advisory Board comprising regional agricultural and environmental experts, senior professionals from partner organizations, community leaders, academics, and policy advisors.

This governance structure ensures our platform's strategic direction is informed, credible, inclusive, regionally relevant, and responsive to the evolving needs of the communities we serve.
