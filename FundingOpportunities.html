Donor & Funder Profiles

Detailed information about key institutions funding regenerative agriculture, climate resilience, and community development in South and Southeast Asia.

USAID

U.S. Agency for International Development

Strategic Focus

USAID is a leading bilateral donor funding agriculture, environment, and climate programs globally. Key relevant programs include Feed the Future (food security and climate-smart agriculture) and climate adaptation initiatives under the Global Climate Strategy.

Typical Grant Size

Large development projects (often $5–50+ million) through country-specific programs and global initiatives. Smaller grants available through innovation funds like DIV (Development Innovation Ventures).

Funding Mechanisms

Contracts and cooperative agreements issued via solicitations (Annual Program Statements, Requests for Applications) on grants.gov. USAID's Climate Adaptation projects in Asia have included grants to NGOs for locally-led resilience building.

Eligibility

Typically NGOs, universities, or firms (including local partners) with significant capacity; smaller local organizations often participate via sub-grants or partnerships.

Resources

Funding opportunities are published on Grants.gov and USAID Mission websites. The USAID DIV program has a dedicated apply page. USAID provides detailed guidelines for applicants (e.g., the USAID Automated Directives System and specific RFA instructions).

FCDO

UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office

Strategic Focus

The UK government funds international development with priority themes of climate change, poverty alleviation, and inclusive growth. FCDO has supported programs like UK Aid Direct grants for NGOs and the UK PACT (Partnering for Accelerated Climate Transitions) for climate initiatives.

Typical Grant Size

Historically, UK Aid Direct offered Community Partnership grants (~£250k) and Impact grants (£250k–£4m) to NGOs, though such central funds have been under review in recent years. New funding is often announced through FCDO's Funding Finder on GOV.UK.

Eligibility

International and local NGOs, research institutions, and sometimes businesses (for challenge funds).

Resources

The UK contributes to multilateral funds (e.g., Green Climate Fund, CGIAR). FCDO often provides application guidance documents for its funding calls. Contact via FCDO country offices or the UK Aid Direct helpdesk (when active).

IFAD

International Fund for Agricultural Development

Strategic Focus

IFAD is a UN specialized agency and international financial institution that provides grants and low-interest loans for rural development. Besides its loan-funded country projects, IFAD has a grants program focusing on innovative approaches to inclusive agriculture. IFAD's strategic priorities emphasize climate resilience and youth in agriculture.

Typical Grant Size

Regular grants are often $0.5–3 million for multi-country or innovative projects. IFAD issues competitive calls for these grants (published on its website's Calls for Proposals section).

Grant Cycles

Periodic – calls are launched throughout the year tied to specific themes or initiatives.

Eligibility

International or regional organizations, research institutes, and consortia (must have the capacity to implement multi-country projects).

Resources

IFAD provides Terms of Reference and templates with each call (e.g., concept note and budget forms). Queries can be emailed to the contacts provided in call announcements. General information at IFAD Grants.

Rockefeller Foundation

Strategic Focus

Rockefeller Foundation has a strong focus on food systems, climate and resilience, and energy. In Asia, Rockefeller has launched initiatives to advance regenerative agriculture transitions and sustainable diets, supporting collaborations for regenerative farming and promoting climate resilience in agriculture.

Typical Grant Size

Medium to large grants (often $200k – $1M for single projects; larger multi-year grants for major initiatives).

Grantmaking Style

Mix of challenge competitions (e.g., Food Vision Prize awarded $2M across top ideas) and direct grants to programs aligned with their strategy. Rockefeller often works with intermediary partners to issue calls or funds competition platforms.

Eligibility

NGOs, research institutions, social enterprises.

Resources

The foundation publishes open RFPs on its website when available. It also shares impact stories and case studies of successful grantees. Contact via proposals@rockefellerfoundation.org for inquiries.

Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

Strategic Focus

Gates Foundation is a major philanthropic funder of agriculture development research and interventions, with a primary focus on improving crop yields, livelihoods, and climate resilience for small farmers in Africa and South Asia. The foundation's Agricultural Development program invests in grants to CGIAR centers, national research programs, and scale-up of innovations.

Grant Size & Process

Gates Foundation grants are usually large (from hundreds of thousands to multi-million USD), awarded through a strategic invitation process or Grand Challenges calls. Grand Challenges competitions (some co-sponsored by Gates) occasionally include topics like climate-smart agriculture, with initial grants of around $100K.

Eligibility

Non-profits, research institutions, NGOs, sometimes for-profit social enterprises.

Resources

Unsolicited proposals are accepted via an Idea submission portal on the foundation's website, but most grants are made proactively. Grant opportunity announcements (when open) appear on the Gates Foundation grants page.

Walmart Foundation

Strategic Focus

Walmart's philanthropic arm has made a significant commitment to sustainable agriculture in India and other countries. In 2018, it pledged $25 million over 5 years to support India's smallholder farmers – a target it exceeded, investing $39 million across 24 grant projects reaching over 800,000 farmers by 2023.

Focus Areas

Building market access and resilience for smallholders, with a strong emphasis on women farmers. Walmart Foundation grants fund NGOs that organize farmers into producer organizations, train them in sustainable farming methods, and link them to markets.

Grant Size

Typically $1–3 million per project to established NGOs.

Eligibility & Process

Grants are awarded through invited proposals and occasional RFPs. The foundation often issues press releases for new grants but does not have an open application portal – NGOs are usually identified in advance.

Resources

Interested organizations can refer to Walmart's Grant Eligibility and Program Guidelines on their website and reach out via the listed contacts for their focus areas (e.g., community resilience).

Omnivore

Agritech Venture Capital

Strategic Focus

An India-based impact venture capital firm that "funds entrepreneurs building the future of agriculture and food systems." Omnivore has pioneered agritech investing in India with a focus on climate-smart agriculture – explicitly seeking startups catalyzing climate resilience in farming.

Investment Size

Omnivore manages multiple funds (Fund III underway with $215M) and typically invests in seed to Series B stage, with ticket sizes from ~$0.5M to $5M.

Eligibility

For-profit startups with innovative solutions in agrifood tech. NGOs and social enterprises developing innovative tech or business models might seek venture-style grants from such impact VCs.

Resources

Omnivore publishes research on the agritech ecosystem and occasionally collaborates in acceleration programs. They have a clear mandate on agrifood tech in South Asia and encourage pitches aligned to their themes (farmer platforms, IoT/automation for farms, biotech, etc.).

Acumen

Strategic Focus

Acumen is a global non-profit impact investment fund active in South Asia and Africa for decades. Climate-resilient agriculture is one of Acumen's core portfolios – "helping farmers and rural families increase incomes to withstand climate shocks by investing in cutting-edge solutions like distributed renewables and regenerative agriculture."

Investment Size

Acumen's investments range from agribusinesses providing drought-tolerant seeds or solar irrigation, to innovative farmer service companies. Typically in the $250k–$3M range.

Support Beyond Capital

Beyond capital, Acumen runs accelerators and provides technical assistance. For example, the India Climate Resilient Agriculture Accelerator was launched to mentor ag-tech startups in 2021.

Eligibility

For investment – scalable for-profit social enterprises with viable business models in target countries; for accelerators – early-stage ventures (often pre-investment).

Resources

Acumen's website has information for social entrepreneurs and the "Acumen Academy" runs open calls for accelerator cohort applications.

Research-Specific Funding Opportunities

Funding opportunities geared toward research and innovation in regenerative agriculture, soil health, water management, and biodiversity – for both academics and practitioner-researchers.

Asia-Pacific Network for Global Change Research (APN)

Region: Asia-Pacific (including all focus countries)
Grant Size: Up to ~USD $100,000 for CRRP research projects
Cycle: Annual – calls open in Q4

APN is a regional science network that offers grants for climate change and sustainable development research involving Asian institutions. It issues an annual call for proposals under two programs: the Collaborative Regional Research Programme (CRRP) and the Capacity Development Programme (CAPaBLE).

Topics encompass climate change adaptation, disaster risk reduction, "smart" agriculture, water quality, biodiversity, etc., with specific priority themes for South Asia and Southeast Asia each cycle. Research teams from APN member countries can apply, with collaboration across at least 2 countries usually required. Early-career scientists are encouraged to lead.

CGIAR Research Initiatives

Region: Global with focus on developing countries
Grant Size: Varies by program, can range from $100K to several million

CGIAR, a global partnership of agricultural research centers (like IRRI, CIMMYT, ICRAF, etc.), often engages in collaborative projects and sometimes provides grants to partners. While CGIAR's core research is in-house, some programs have competitive grant components.

For example, the former CGIAR Research Program on Climate Change, Agriculture and Food Security (CCAFS) periodically issued grants for pilot climate-smart villages and research-in-development projects. Another example is the CGIAR Big Data "Inspire Challenge", which from 2017–2021 awarded $100,000 grants to winning proposals leveraging data for agricultural development.

In the One CGIAR era (post-2022), there are Innovation Challenges and Accelerator programs under initiatives like Digital Innovation. To engage, connect with CGIAR research initiatives (e.g., CGIAR Initiatives on Sustainable Intensification, ClimBeR for climate resilience) – these often have partnership funds to sub-grant to local research/NGO partners.

Innovation Fund

Development Innovation Ventures (DIV)

USAID

Region: Global (low and lower-middle income countries)
Cycle: Continuous (open year-round)

USAID's open innovation fund backs breakthrough development solutions through a staged finance model. Stage 1 grants offer up to $500,000 for pilots, with larger awards (up to several million) for testing and scaling proven solutions. Open to any entity with evidence-backed innovations in USAID-supported countries.
