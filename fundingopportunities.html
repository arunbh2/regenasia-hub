Regenerative Fund for Nature

Conservation International, Kering & Inditex

Region: Global (includes India)
Cycle: Annual (2024 closed, next expected 2025)

Established in 2021 to transform 1 million hectares of farms by 2026 for regenerative production of key materials (leather, cotton, wool, cashmere). Provides grants to farming groups, NGOs, and project leaders implementing practices that improve soil health, biodiversity, and farmer livelihoods.

Region: Asia-Pacific

Private Sector for Food Systems (PS4FS)

International Fund for Agricultural Development (IFAD)

Region: South & Southeast Asia (6 countries)
Status: Closed August 2024 (similar calls expected)

A competitive grant aimed at partnering with the private sector to benefit smallholder farmers. Funds projects to stimulate investments in inclusive, climate-resilient value chains across six target countries (Cambodia, India, Laos, Philippines, Sri Lanka, Vietnam). Multi-year funding in the range of USD $3-5 million.

GEF Small Grants Programme (SGP)

United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)

Region: Global (all focus countries eligible)
Cycle: Rolling (ongoing)

Provides grants up to USD $50,000 directly to local communities and civil society organizations for projects that "conserve and restore the environment while enhancing people's well-being and livelihoods." Since 1992, SGP has embodied the "think globally, act locally" approach to sustainable development.

Focus Areas

Innovation Fund Continuous

Development Innovation Ventures (DIV)

USAID

Region: Global (low and lower-middle income countries)
Cycle: Continuous (open year-round)

USAID's open innovation fund backs breakthrough development solutions through a staged finance model. Stage 1 grants offer up to $500,000 for pilots, with larger awards (up to several million) for testing and scaling proven solutions. Open to any entity with evidence-backed innovations in USAID-supported countries.

Impact Fund Periodic Windows

Global Innovation Fund (GIF)

Multi-donor Impact Fund

Region: Global (developing countries)
Cycle: Periodic application windows

GIF supports social innovations in developing countries that can improve lives of people living on under $5/day. It offers grants and risk-tolerant capital ranging from pilot funding (~$50k–$250k) to scaling ($1–15 million). Open to NGOs, for-profit companies, researchers with transformative innovations aligned to GIF's criteria.

Agricultural Technology Climate Adaptation Financial Inclusion Poverty Reduction

Awards & Grants Annual

Ashden Awards

Ashden

Region: Global (specific categories for Asia/Africa)
Cycle: Annual (applications typically open Q4, close Q1)

The Ashden Awards recognize innovative climate solutions, including a dedicated Award for Regenerative Agriculture. Winners receive a cash grant (typically £10,000 or more), international publicity, and business support. Open to enterprises, NGOs, and programs demonstrating impact in sustainable energy, natural climate solutions, and regenerative farming.
