Practical resources, learning modules, and certifications to build skills in regenerative agriculture

Practical Toolkits

Ready-to-use guides and resources for implementing regenerative practices

Soil Health Toolkit Manual (CCRP)

English, Hindi, Thai PDF Format

Developed by the Collaborative Crop Research Program, this toolkit details simple field methods to monitor soil structure, infiltration, earthworm counts, and other indicators, enabling practitioners to gauge improvements from regenerative practices. Particularly useful for NGOs working with farmers to measure progress in soil health restoration.

Soil Testing Monitoring Methods Farmer Assessment
Download Toolkit

FAO Field Guide to Improve Water Use Efficiency

English, Vietnamese, Nepali PDF Format

This practical field guide from FAO provides strategies for extension workers and farmers on selecting appropriate irrigation methods, scheduling irrigation to avoid waste, and implementing simple water-saving practices like mulching and rainwater harvesting. The guide is full of illustrations on building small catchments and calibrating irrigation systems.

Irrigation Efficiency Rainwater Harvesting Water Conservation
Download Toolkit

Agroforestry in Rice Landscapes (FAO & ICRAF)

English, Thai, Indonesian, Filipino PDF Format

This Southeast Asia-focused manual outlines step-by-step how extension workers can help farmers design and implement agroforestry systems in rice-growing areas. It includes guidance on assessing farm needs, selecting species (with tables of suitable trees for various countries), nursery management, planting techniques, and maintenance. Uses decision trees for choosing between windbreaks, woodlots, or intercropping based on farmer objectives.

Tree Selection Multi-strata Design Rice-Tree Integration
Download Toolkit

Farmer Field School (FFS) Implementer's Guide

English, Hindi, Sinhala PDF Format

Developed by FAO, this guide explains how to facilitate season-long farmer trainings with discovery-based learning. It includes modules for group dynamics, setting up demonstration plots, and encouraging farmer experimentation. Many NGOs in Asia have adapted FFS for climate-smart agriculture, combining soil health, climate risk mapping, and action planning in their curricula.

Farmer Field Schools Participatory Learning Group Facilitation
Download Toolkit

Resilience Design in Smallholder Farming Systems

English, Thai PDF Format

Developed by the International Water Management Institute (IWMI), this toolkit integrates water management into farm design. It helps farmers map their farm's slope, water flows, and resource zones, then plan structures like swales or retention basins. Includes checklists and monitoring forms to measure improvements in water availability and crop performance after implementing "resilience design" features.

Farm Design Water Harvesting Climate Resilience
Download Toolkit

Community Seed Bank Starter Kit

English, Hindi, Nepali PDF Format

Developed by Bioversity International (now part of the Alliance of Bioversity-CIAT), this guide covers establishing and managing community seed banks. It includes guidance on involving the community in seed saving, proper storage techniques, documentation of seed varieties, and legal aspects of seed exchange. In Nepal and India, NGOs have successfully used this to empower villages to conserve climate-resilient crop varieties.

Seed Saving Biodiversity Community Management
Download Toolkit

Certifications

Structured learning paths to build and verify your expertise

Regenerative Agriculture Practitioner

Foundational

This certification provides a solid foundation in regenerative agriculture principles and basic implementation techniques suitable for farmers, extension workers, and agricultural professionals.

Duration: 3 months (part-time)
Format: Online with practical assignments
Prerequisites: None

Core Modules:

  • Principles of Regenerative Agriculture
  • Soil Biology and Health
  • Water Cycle Management
  • Biodiversity Integration
  • Farm Planning Basics
Learn More & Enroll

Regenerative Systems Designer

Advanced

This certification develops advanced skills in designing, implementing, and managing integrated regenerative agriculture systems at farm and landscape scales.

Duration: 6 months (part-time)
Format: Blended learning with in-person workshops
Prerequisites: Practitioner certification or equivalent experience

Core Modules:

  • Whole Systems Design
  • Advanced Agroforestry Systems
  • Integrated Water Management
  • Climate Resilience Strategies
  • Monitoring & Adaptive Management
  • Farm Economics & Value Chains
Learn More & Enroll

Master Trainer in Regenerative Agriculture

Expert

This certification prepares experienced practitioners to effectively train others in regenerative agriculture principles and practices through participatory learning approaches.

Duration: 4 months (intensive)
Format: In-person training with field practicums
Prerequisites: Systems Designer certification or significant field experience

Core Modules:

  • Adult Learning Principles
  • Facilitating Farmer Field Schools
  • Participatory Research Methods
  • Curriculum Development
  • Monitoring & Evaluation
  • Community Mobilization
