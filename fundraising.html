Featured initiatives making real impact through regenerative agriculture

Philippines

Mindanao Coffee Agroforestry Transition

Building on successful models from the Nestlé NESCAFÉ Plan in Mindanao, this project supports 500 smallholder coffee growers to implement regenerative agroforestry systems by planting shade trees and cover crops in coffee fields, using compost instead of chemical fertilizer, and intercropping with fruit and timber species for diversification of income streams.

50,000 trees planted
500 families supported
1,200 hectares regenerated

India

Carbon Sequestration in Dryland Farming Systems

Building on ICAR research showing increases in soil organic carbon of 0.1–0.3% per year on regenerative plots, this project measures and enhances carbon sequestration in dryland India. Through no-till, cover crops, and agroforestry practices, we aim to quantify climate mitigation benefits while improving farm resilience to drought through enhanced soil moisture retention and diversified cropping systems.

5-year scientific study
10 demonstration sites
Quantified carbon benefits
View Details Support Project
Nepal

Women-Led Climate-Resilient Agriculture Network

Research by IFPRI's Gender and Climate Resilient Agriculture study in South Asia shows that farms where women have more say tend to diversify crops more and invest in soil conservation. This project empowers women farmers in Nepal's vulnerable mountain communities through training in water harvesting, agroforestry, and terrace management specifically adapted to hill farming systems, with peer mentoring and demonstration plots.

200 women farmer leaders
20 demo farms established
Reaching 2,000+ households
View Details Support Project
Bangladesh

Saline-Tolerant Rice Varieties and Floating Gardens

This research project tackles the dual challenges of flooding and salinization in Bangladesh's coastal regions. Building on traditional floating garden techniques (locally known as "dhap" or "baira"), the project combines these indigenous methods with trials of salt-tolerant rice varieties to create climate-adaptive agriculture systems. These approaches allow continued production even during prolonged flooding, while reclaiming lands affected by saltwater intrusion.

12 rice varieties trialed
Flood-resilient techniques
350 farmers participating

Mobile Soil Testing Labs

21 days left

Inspired by FAO's Soil Doctors Programme, this project provides mobile soil testing units for farmers in remote areas of Thailand. Building on Unilever's Jasmine Rice Project where soil testing enabled farmers to reduce fertilizer use by 30-40%, these labs will offer affordable testing for pH, organic matter, and nutrients, along with guidance on regenerative soil management practices.

Regenerative Rice Farmer Field Schools

36 days left

Using FAO's Farmer Field School methodology, this campaign will train rice farmers in Vietnam's Mekong Delta on alternate wetting and drying (AWD) techniques that can cut water use by ~30% and methane emissions by ~50% without reducing yield. Training will also cover cover crops in fallow periods, following the model of Thailand's Jasmine Rice Project that saw improved flood and drought tolerance.
